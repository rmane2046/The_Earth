RIVERS

Rivers are the lifelines of Earth's ecosystems, carving through continents and shaping landscapes over millions of years. Originating from glaciers, springs, or rainfall, rivers flow across vast terrains, nourishing forests, sustaining wildlife, and supporting human civilizations along their banks. From the powerful Amazon to the sacred Ganges, these flowing waters have influenced cultures, economies, and natural habitats. Rivers play a crucial role in the water cycle, transporting nutrients, sediments, and freshwater to oceans and lakes. As dynamic forces of nature, they continue to shape valleys and deltas while offering beauty, biodiversity, and essential resources for life on Earth.
